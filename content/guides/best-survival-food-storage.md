## What to look for

### 1) Pantry rotation vs long‑term storage
- Rotation pantry: convenience + access
- Long‑term: moisture + oxygen + pests

### 2) What you’re storing
Dry goods store differently than oils/fats. Match the method to the food type.

### 3) Moisture & oxygen control
For long‑term storage, these are the real enemies.

### 4) Practicality beats perfection
A simple system you rotate is better than a perfect system you abandon.

## A realistic plan (starter)

### Week 1
Build a 7–14 day pantry buffer of what you already eat. Label purchase dates.

### Month 1
Add long‑term staples (dry goods). Use mylar + buckets if you want longer stability.

### Ongoing
Set a monthly rotation day. Replace what you use.

## Common mistakes
1) Buying food you don’t eat
2) No labeling
3) Ignoring moisture

## FAQ

### Simplest way to start?
Buy extra of what you already eat and rotate it.

### Are mylar bags required?
Not for short‑term rotation. For long‑term dry goods, they help when used correctly.

### Prevent pests?
Airtight containers + correct storage practices.

### Freeze‑dried meals?
Convenient but expensive. Many people use them as a supplement.
